\section{Methods}

For each nuclear receptor, ligand-bound crystal structures and relevant literature were reviewed to identify pocket residues and ligand--receptor interactions associated with known agonists. These interactions were inspected in \textit{MolSoft ICM-Pro 3.9.5} \cite{abagyan1994icm} and used to interpret docking poses of compounds 681 and 889. To account for receptor flexibility, docking was performed using ensembles of structurally diverse ligand-bound conformations \cite{bottegoni2009four,guedes2014receptor,park2010improved,rueda2010recipes,totrov2008flexible}, selected from the literature or identified using ProteinPlus and SIENA \cite{bietz2016siena,schoning2020proteins} or using the NR-DBIND dataset \cite{reau2018nuclear}. Candidate structures were screened for crystallographic and ligand-modelling quality using StructureProfiler \cite{friedrich2017high,hartshorn2007diverse,meyder2019structureprofiler,warren2012essential}. Co-crystallised ligands were redocked to assess pose reproduction and provide receptor-specific reference scores. Compounds 681 and 889 were then evaluated using docking score, receptor conformation, visual similarity to reference ligands, and consistency with known interaction patterns \cite{errington2025assessing,fischer2021decision}.
